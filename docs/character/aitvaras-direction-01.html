Aitvaras · Character Direction 01 · July 2026

A presence, not a mascot.

Aitvaras is rendered as a hologram — a figure made of light, not of skin or cartoon shading. Human enough to feel like someone; abstract enough to never be creepy or cutesy. She is calm by default and communicates through light and motion: how she glows is how she feels.

Rationale

Why a figure of light

Serious, not sterile

A monochrome emissive palette and a restrained silhouette read as competence. No big eyes, no bouncing — warmth comes from soft light, breathing motion, and a slow blink.

States are the language

Idle dims her. Listening pulls the halo tight and brightens her eyes. Thinking sends current through the halo. Speaking makes the whole figure resonate with her voice. You can read her status from across the room.

Built for the app

Emissive particles and lines are cheap to render in RealityKit, scale from a tiny floating companion window to full screen, and look native on a dark desktop. No rigging, no uncanny valley, no asset pipeline.

In the app, the hologram hue can shift subtly with context — amber tint when she surfaces something urgent, dimmed teal when idle in the corner of your screen. In RealityKit this becomes instanced emissive quads plus a bloom pass; every state above is a small parameter curve (brightness, halo radius, particle energy), driven by the same state machine the voice pipeline uses.

Feedback

What to react to

  • Silhouette — more or less recognizably feminine? Hair shape longer/shorter?
  • Treatment A, B, or C — or A with a hint of B's lines?
  • Color — stay teal, or explore ice-blue / violet territory?
  • Motion pace — is idle calm enough? Is speaking lively enough?
  • Density — more solid (readable) vs. more sparse (ethereal)?
